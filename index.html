That question has a practical answer. It is not complicated. It just requires someone to give it to you straight.

The Background

Built by someone who ran the meetings, not the models.

Sans Noise was founded by Sami Ammous, a business leader with two decades of experience running sales organizations, coaching executives, and advising companies on growth across the Asia-Pacific region.

In that work, one pattern repeated: every conversation eventually found its way back to AI. The concerns were real. The information available was not particularly useful. Sami built Sans Noise to close that gap, with a structured day that gives senior leaders direct experience of the tools, an honest picture of the landscape, and a way of thinking about organizational decisions that they can use the following week.

Sami writes regularly on LinkedIn.

The Offer

Built for leaders who make decisions, not those who study them.

The Executive Brief is the only program Sans Noise offers, built around one belief: senior leaders need more detail than the headlines but less than the code.

It is a full-day, in-person workshop for small groups of senior leaders, four to eight participants from the same organization. Participants work with AI directly, using real scenarios rather than case studies. The facilitator is also the program designer. And because the group comes from the same organization, the afternoon's discussion about where AI is worth pursuing in the business is grounded in shared context, not hypotheticals.

This is not a keynote, a lunch-and-learn, or a vendor demonstration. It is a working session with a defined output: leaders who know what to do next, and why.

The Program

The Executive Brief runs in three parts.

01

Reading the landscape accurately

An honest, non-technical account of where AI actually is today: what it can and cannot do, how the products most participants will encounter actually work, and how to evaluate the claims that land on your desk. Participants leave with a permanent filter, not a summary they will have forgotten by Thursday.

02

AI as a working tool

Hands-on exercises built around real work: thinking and decision support, communications, analysis and synthesis. The goal is not awareness of what AI can do. It is the prompting instinct that turns the tool from a novelty into a daily habit.

03

Organizational judgment

A practical framework for any AI decision: how to identify where AI is actually worth pursuing, how to sequence initiatives, and how to evaluate a vendor claim or a budget proposal without outsourcing the judgment to the vendor. The foundation for every enterprise AI conversation that follows.

The Outcome

Within a few weeks, participants are consistently doing two things differently.

In their own work

They use AI regularly for thinking support, drafting, and analysis. They apply sharper judgment to AI news and vendor claims. They ask better questions when facing novel problems.

In their organizations

They have identified the two or three areas most worth serious attention. They have a framework for evaluating any initiative or tool that lands on their desk. And they can lead that assessment themselves, without handing the judgment to a consultant.
